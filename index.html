Introduction

The Story of a Real Man is a book by Boris Polevoy. He is the author of books about war. For example... Once, when the fame of Alexey Meresyev spread around the area, the reporter Boris Polevoy hurried to hear his story. During the story, he wrote it down in a notebook. At the end of the story, in an additional chapter, there is a reference to the history of the creation of the story. It is also included in the plot.

Plot

The story is about a pilot who crashed during the war. He found himself alone in the middle of the forest. At the very beginning, he was attacked by a bear, but he won. Due to the lack of cold, his legs were frostbitten. He crawled more than 100 kilometers for a month. When the allies met him, he was taken to hospital. There he was treated for about 3 months. His legs were amputated. Afterwards he was discharged from the hospital, found love and was able to prove that even with prosthetics he is able to fly a plane. The main idea of ​​the story is to show the difficulties of people in war and who they can become.

Interesting facts

1. The plot is based on events that happened in the life of the Soviet ace pilot Alexey Maresyev 2. The book was published more than eighty times in Russian, forty-nine times in the languages ​​of the peoples of the USSR, thirty-nine times abroad. 3. Alexey Maresyev (the prototype of the main character) made 86 combat sorties during the war, shot down 11 enemy aircraft: four before being wounded and seven after being wounded. 4. The book was written in 1946. 5. Three years later, after the end of the war, the correspondent returned to the idea of ​​creating a work about the feat of the legless pilot. 6. The work is imbued with humanism and Soviet patriotism. In the USSR, the book "The Story of a Real Man" was part of the school literature curriculum.
